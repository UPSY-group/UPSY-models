Title: MISMIP+

Keywords:
- model intercomparison
- grounding-line migration
- idealised geometry

URLs:
- https://doi.org/10.5194/gmd-9-2471-2016
- https://doi.org/10.5194/tc-14-2283-2020

Description:
MISMIP+ describes a large ice stream flowing down a laterally symmetric
valley, into a confined embayment. In the initial steady-state geometry,
the grounding line rests on a retrograde slope, and is kept in place
by a strongly buttressing shelf. In the ice1r retreat experiment, a warm
ocean is prescribed, causing the shelf to thin and the grounding line to
retreat. In the test, we directly compare UFEMISM's modelled grounding-line
position over time to the model ensemble by Cornford et al. (2020).
